# 🧠 Direction 4: Subjective Well-being & Stress Tracker

Back to Directions: [[Directions Overview|Directions Overview]] | Back to MOC: [[Hackathon MOC]]

This direction adapts our fourth concept into the teammate's 4-field proposal template.

---

### 💡 The Unique Angle
**Underwriting the mind, not just the money.** Most microfinance tools react when a client defaults. This tool works like a psychological early warning system, tracking the emotional and mental stress of debt to prevent over-indebtedness before it happens.
	
---

### ⚙️ Concept
An interactive conversational survey tool integrated into loan officers' weekly group meetings. Through simple, culturally relevant voice checks, it gathers qualitative sentiment data on financial control, anxiety, and household safety. An NLP sentiment model analyzes responses to calculate a **Subjective Stress Index**. If stress spikes, the system automatically alerts the loan officer to offer constructive care plans (such as a repayment holiday or savings match).

---

### 🚀 Why It's Different
Traditional credit scoring models only track transaction numbers (balances, defaults) and ignore domestic dynamics or psychological distress. This model **shifts the MFI-client dynamic from transactional collection to relational support**. It recognizes that a client with a 100% repayment rate may still be in a state of high debt stress or experiencing domestic abuse, allowing pre-emptive intervention.

---

### ⚠️ Risk
Gathering subjective mental and emotional health data raises significant ethical and privacy concerns. FSPs must handle this data strictly to support borrowers, ensuring it is never weaponized to deny services.
