# 🏪 Direction 2: Voice-Led Micro-Ledger for Retailers

Back to Directions: [[Directions Overview|Directions Overview]] | Back to MOC: [[Hackathon MOC]]

This direction adapts our second concept into the teammate's 4-field proposal template.

---

### 💡 The Unique Angle
**Zero-UI bookkeeping.** Instead of forcing microfinance clients to navigate complex interfaces and type numbers, we let them track their finances by talking to a WhatsApp or Messenger chatbot as if they were talking to a friend, speaking in local languages or mixed slang (e.g., Taglish or Javanese).

---

### ⚙️ Concept
A messaging-based AI assistant. A merchant records a quick voice message (e.g., *"I bought 3 cases of soda for 900 pesos"*). An Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) engine transcribes it, and a fine-tuned LLM parses it into structured JSON objects (amount, item, category, transaction type). Weekly, the bot delivers automated audio financial health diagnoses (e.g., *"Your margin is up 5%, consider setting aside 100 pesos for savings"*).

---

### 🚀 Why It's Different
Standard bookkeeping tools require manual data entry, which fails due to digital illiteracy and fatigue. This solution **eliminates digital literacy bottlenecks entirely** by shifting the interface from screens and forms to conversational speech. It meets merchants on the messaging apps they already use daily, offering direct financial advisory instead of just raw charts.

---

### ⚠️ Risk
Background noise in busy public markets can disrupt speech recognition accuracy. The ASR engine requires robust training on low-resource dialects and hybrid slangs.
