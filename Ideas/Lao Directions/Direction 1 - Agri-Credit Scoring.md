# 🌾 Direction 1: Alternative-Data Agri-Credit Scoring

Back to Directions: [[Directions Overview|Directions Overview]] | Back to MOC: [[Hackathon MOC]]

This direction adapts our first concept into the teammate's 4-field proposal template.

---
### 💡 The Unique Angle
Instead of trying to force offline smallholder farmers to build credit histories through digital payments they don't make, we **translate their physical environment and community ties into underwriting signals**. We digitize their existing offline records and evaluate the earth they farm to determine creditworthiness.

---

### ⚙️ Concept
A mobile-first workflow tool for cooperative field agents to photograph farmers' handwritten logs. An OCR + Small Language Model (SLM) backend extracts transaction ledgers. This is combined with local satellite vegetation indices (NDVI), weather risk feeds, and cooperative peer trust scores (meeting attendance, group savings) to generate a **Unified Farm Resilience & Credit Score** displayed on a loan officer's dashboard.

---

### 🚀 Why It's Different
Most credit-scoring solutions focus on e-commerce, mobile money transactions, or utility bills. This model works at the **intersection of climate resilience, computer vision, and agricultural alternative data**. It turns offline, real-world assets (crops, physical ledgers, community reputation) into structured underwriting insights, moving credit scoring from urban tech hubs to rural cooperatives.

---

### ⚠️ Risk
High operational reliance on field agents' discipline to take readable, consistent photos of messy handwritten logs. Low-resource OCR handwriting models may require manual validation if transcription confidence is low.
