# 🌦️ Direction 3: Parametric Micro-Insurance Registry

Back to Directions: [[Directions Overview|Directions Overview]] | Back to MOC: [[Hackathon MOC]]

This direction adapts our third concept into the teammate's 4-field proposal template.

---

### 💡 The Unique Angle
**Instant, zero-claims disaster recovery.** It transforms disaster relief and micro-insurance from a slow, bureaucratic claims-adjustment nightmare into an automated, transparent data utility. Payouts are triggered by physical reality, not paperwork.

---

### ⚙️ Concept
A shared data registry mapping climate-vulnerable farmers/fisherfolk plots. It links weather APIs and satellite wind/rainfall datasets directly to a rules engine. When wind speeds (typhoons) or rainfall levels (floods/droughts) cross objective thresholds, the platform automatically instructs local mobile money services (e.g., GCash, bKash) to disburse immediate relief payouts to affected communities within 48 hours.

---

### 🚀 Why It's Different
Traditional micro-insurance is crippled by the "trust gap" and high administrative costs; inspectors must physically verify crop damage, delaying payouts for months when families need them most. This tool **sits at the intersection of parametric climate indexes, API-driven registries, and local mobile money networks**, triggering objective, binary payouts with zero operational friction.

---

### ⚠️ Risk
**Basis Risk**: The risk that a farmer experiences localized damage but the satellite grids or regional weather stations do not hit the official parametric trigger threshold, leading to no payout.
