
------------------


NOTE THAT THEY REPEATEDLY EMPHASIZE "UNDERSERVED COMMUNITIES"

Examples include:

- low-income families
- rural populations
- informal workers
- gig workers
- migrant workers
- women with limited financial access
- people without traditional credit histories
- young adults with poor financial literacy

The emphasis is on financial inclusion rather than serving customers who already have access to sophisticated financial products.

------------------------

THEY R NOT LOOKING FOR ANOTHER BUDGETING SHI APP

This is important.

Many hackathons receive submissions like:

- expense tracker
- savings app
- AI chatbot
- budget planner

Those are common ideas.

This hackathon is focused on **system-level impact**. The organizers are interested in solutions that help financial institutions identify, understand, and support customers more effectively—not just another consumer-facing finance app.

-----------

READING BETWEEN THE LINES

Although not explicitly stated as judging criteria, the repeated themes suggest they are looking for solutions that are:

- **AI-powered**, where AI adds meaningful value rather than being included for its own sake.
- **Data-driven**, making effective use of financial or alternative data.
- **Responsible**, with attention to privacy, fairness, transparency, and bias.
- **Practical**, capable of being adopted by an actual financial institution.
- **Scalable**, applicable across APAC rather than only one local setting.
- **Inclusive**, improving outcomes for financially underserved populations
