## 🔍 The Core Problem

Mindanao — and APAC more broadly — sits inside one of the world's most active typhoon belts. Floods, droughts, and storms don't just damage crops; they devastate the fragile financial lives of smallholder farmers and coastal communities who have **no buffer and no advance warning system**.

Most FSPs know their clients are climate-vulnerable. But their response is purely reactive: wait for disaster to happen, then deal with the flood of defaults, loan restructuring requests, and emergency disbursement bottlenecks.

The challenge is: **FSPs cannot pre-position emergency financial products or identify at-risk members before a climate shock hits, because they have no system connecting climate data to member livelihood profiles.**

---

## 📌 Why This Is Hard

| Obstacle | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **No livelihood-climate mapping** | FSPs record "loan purpose" (e.g., "farming") but rarely know the specific crop type, plot location, or climate exposure level of each borrower. |
| **Weather data is disconnected** | Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration (PAGASA) typhoon data exists publicly, but no MFI has a pipeline connecting it to their loan portfolio. |
| **Relief is always too slow** | Post-disaster credit restructuring requires manual approval chains, field inspections, and bureaucracy — taking weeks when families need days. |
| **Pre-positioning is never done** | No cooperative proactively moves members into emergency savings or insurance products 2 weeks before typhoon season peaks. It only happens after the disaster. |

---

## 🧩 Finverse Categories This Challenge Maps To

1. **[[Finverse Data Access#Limited-Data-Availability-&-Accessibility|Limited Data Availability & Accessibility]]**: Granular geospatial climate risk data exists in government and satellite databases but is never integrated with MFI member portfolios.
2. **[[Finverse Resource Constraints#Lack-of-Timely-or-Granular-Data|Lack of Timely or Granular Data]]**: PAGASA reports are regional and weekly; what MFIs need is plot-level, daily forecasts during typhoon season.
3. **[[Finverse Data Quality#Fragmented-&-Disconnected-Systems|Fragmented & Disconnected Systems]]**: Climate data, member livelihood records, savings data, and insurance coverage are stored in completely separate systems with no integration.

## ❓ Open Questions
- What is the lead time between PAGASA typhoon warnings and actual landfall in Mindanao? Is 48–72 hours enough for pre-positioning?
- Can we integrate PAGASA's [Climate Change in the Philippines](https://www.pagasa.dost.gov.ph) data feeds via API, or will we need to scrape/manually pull data?
- What threshold of wind speed or rainfall constitutes a "parametric trigger" for FSPs in the Mindanao context specifically?
- Has any Philippine cooperative ever used weather index insurance? If so, what were the barriers to adoption?
